UI/UX, Prototyping, Passion project

Harnessing the power of citizen science

Manu is a mobile app that identifies bird songs in the field. Users can upload, tag and share bird sightings, the data collected is used to help aid bird conservation in New Zealand.

Overview

New Zealand birds are unique - they help define us as a nation. We have around 68 species of endemic birds, most of which have distinct songs and calls. However, recognising even a few birds from songs or calls is difficult for most people.

Manu seeks to solve this by bringing people closer to the natural environment through technology and raise awareness of the importance of conservation by providing a fun and dynamic learning experience for all ages.

Manu also enables the crowd-sourcing of data which will aid the bird conservation effort in New Zealand. Each time the app is used to identify a bird, a geotag function will log where each species are located. This data will be publicly available through a website enabling individuals, communities, school groups, conservation groups, scientists and government agencies to monitor individual bird species.

Project objectives
  • Enable New Zealanders to easily identify NZ bird song/calls.
  • Foster greater connection with the natural environment through education and engagement.
  • Provide a useful learning tool in both English and Te Reo (kura kaupapa MƒÅori based schools.)
  • Allow citizens to collect meaningful data to help understand more about our native bird species. This may help inform decisions about the management of indigenous fauna.

User stories

To established clearly what the app had to do I produced a series of user stories based on the target audience. I then created a feature list which I could use as a foundation for wireframes and user flows later on. Because Manu seeks to address many different problems, it would naturally require many features, for now, however, I only wanted to include features necessary for a minimum viable product (MVP).

Testing wireframes

Testing the wireframes was an important step. I wanted to make sure all the elements and flow worked before developing the UI. Using Figma's prototyping feature, I created a low fidelity prototype which I tested and made improvements to the wireframes. After the modifications, I was ready to move on to the interface design.

Testing wireframes using Figma

Visual design exploration

Utilising Figma's artboard feature, I rapidly iterated and experimented with different design solutions and produced hi-fidelity mockups based on the wireframes.

Exploring visual design solutions using Figma

High-fidelity prototype

I wanted Manu to be a very minimalistic app as it is intended to be used by people from a wide range of demographics. The interface design aims to be simple and uncluttered without any unnecessary elements.

Photography is an important part of the app so I didn't want users to be distracted or overhelmed with bright or vibrant colors. I, therefore, decided to choose a neutral colour scheme and choose sapphire blue as the primary colour because not compete with the vibrant colors in the photography and allowed it to stand out more.

This project is still ongoing. My next step is to create an interactive prototype and test it with real users.
